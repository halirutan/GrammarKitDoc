The best docs tend to sound like a **calm, capable teammate**: friendly, practical, and focused on getting the reader unstuck.

* **Conversational, respectful, not cute:** approachable and human, but avoids slang, jokes, and pop culture. ([Google for Developers][1])
* **Warm, helpful, and solution-first:** shows empathy, takes responsibility when things fail, and points to a fix without blaming the user. ([Microsoft Learn][2])
* **Crisp and scannable:** concise, direct, and precise so readers can search and skim fast. ([GitLab Docs][3])
* **Active, present, and reader-addressed:** uses present tense, active voice, simple phrasing, and speaks to “you” (not “we”). ([Kubernetes][4])
* **Global-English friendly:** avoids idioms, jargon, Latin abbreviations, and culture-specific references to keep meaning stable and translatable. ([Kubernetes][4])
